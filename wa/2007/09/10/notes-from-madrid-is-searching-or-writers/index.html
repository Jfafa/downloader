Notes from Madrid is searching for Writers!

10 September, 2007 (10:23) | General

by Ben Curtis

Do you love exploring the city’s finest bars, restaurants, shops, hotels, and secret streets and parks? Notes from Madrid is looking to relaunch this Autumn to become the biggest, most interesting, and most useful Madrid site on the net. We are looking for sharp, fun writers who are based in Madrid and are keen to take part in this exciting project.

If you are interested, send us a sample post that you would be proud to see published here and read by thousands of global travelers.

Important! Pleas note:

- Sample posts must be grammatically correct, a strict maximum of 200 words in length, and must be suited to one of the Notes from Madrid categories listed in the far right column of this page.

- Check out previous posts on these pages for ideas, and bear in mind our tag line: “Where we’d take our friends”

- Extra Kudos will be awarded to posts that include one of your own good quality (pref. 580 pixel wide) photos, or even a short video blog! … post on youtube…

- Sample posts should include address/location details for the place you are writing about

- Writers that join the team must be Madrid-based and should be prepared to submit one to two short (around 200 words) posts a week

- Please include a short bio on yourself

- Input will be paid after a short trial period

Deadline for posts (don’t forget the max word count!) is Monday September 17th, and all entries must be sent to:

ben *at*
notesfrommadrid [.com]

We look forward to hearing from you!
